Github Copilot: Friend Or Foe

Sohan

So you've probably heard about the new, AI-powered Github Copilot And, it's, uh, frankly, frighteningly awesome. I've got mixed feelings here.

For those who don't know, it's "Your AI Pair Programmer". You type in a comment describing what a function will do and Copilot here will write code for that entire function. You can choose between multiple code snippets it wrote and even suggests StackOverflow answers. And, it works on almost everything, and pretty well too. You can kinda see why I'm having mixed feelings about it. As of right now, if you are lucky, you can get access to it through a waitlist.

But is this going to replace programmers? No, not in a long shot. Companies hire programmers because they know how to deconstruct a problem and turn it into code. Programmers know what to copy and where to paste. Although Github Copilot is freakishly accurate. It's not here to replace us but to aid us. Well, the final thoughts are up to you of course.
